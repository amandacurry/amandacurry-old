About

Hello! My name is Amanda. I am a Spanish-born Brit with a passion for technology. I graduated in 2015 from Heriot-Watt University with a degree in Computer Science.

For my undergraduate thesis I developed, implemented and tested different strategies to generate context-dependent navigation instructions based on real human data. I presented some preliminary results at the BCSWomen Lovelace Colloquium and worn first prize and a short paper was published in the ENLG 2015 conference proceedings.

In my latest project I worked with Dr Dimitra Gkatzia and Dr Verena Rieser in collaboration with the MetOffice studying human decision-making when presented with uncertain information about the weather forecast. I developed an online game in which users gave an ice cream seller advice on where to sell his ice-cream based on temperature and precipitation forecasts. The forecasts were presented either graphically or verbally, or with a combination of the two, in order to assess which strategy of data representation would help users understand risks best and thus make the most appropriate decision.

My main areas of interest are Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. I am passionate about interaction design, as I believe good design is key to incorporating technology into our daily lives seamlessly.

In 2016 I will be starting a PhD with Dr Verena Rieser in the area of situated dialogue.
